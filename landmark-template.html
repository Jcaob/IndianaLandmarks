440 Club

Canal Overlook Apartments, 430 Indiana Avenue

Historical:440 Indiana Ave

Use Today:Dental office

During the mid-1940s, a slim young man walked through the door of the 440 Club, one of the dozens of nightclubs supporting an emerging jazz and blues scene on Indiana Avenue. John Leslie Montgomery, who went by the name “Wes,” was responding to an ad with a simple request: “Talent Wanted.” Years later, jazz lovers around the world would come to recognize Montgomery as one of the greatest jazz guitarists who ever lived.

Chapter 1

John Leslie Montgomery

By the late 1960s, he had been honored with two Grammy Awards and several Grammy Award nominations. Throughout his career, he also was recognized with numerous other recognitions, including Record World’s Jazzman of the Year by Record World and Billboard Magazine’s Most Promising Jazz Instrumentalist.


But the road to fame was paved with hard work. As a teen, Montgomery, who was born in Indianapolis on March 6, 1923, spent hours practicing on the four-string tenor guitar that his older brother, Monk, had bought him from a pawn shop. Years later, Wes recalled, he had to learn to play all over again when he was able to purchase his first six-string guitar. Many nights, out of concern that he would wake his family and neighbors with his practices, Montgomery would play with his thumb instead of a pick. That style of playing, along with the use of octaves in his jazz improvisations. eventually became his trademark.
